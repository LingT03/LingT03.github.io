Research assistantship across three laboratories in the **Department of
Biomedical Informatics**, contributing computational and statistical
analyses spanning network biology, regression-based disease staging, and
genomic association studies.

### Janani Ravi Lab — Assistant Professor, Biomedical Informatics

- Applied **network-based machine-learning models** to identify novel
  disease-associated genes, initially focused on tuberculosis.
- Conducted **cross-disease analyses** to uncover shared genes and
  pathways across cystic fibrosis / IPF (genetic), COPD (environmental),
  and asthma (autoimmune).
- Translated computational findings into actionable insights through
  collaborative interdisciplinary work.

### Katerina Kechris Lab — Associate Director of Data Science

- Performed **EDA, cleaning, and feature preparation** on the COPDGene
  dataset for FEV1 regression and GOLD-stage classification.
- Designed, trained, and fine-tuned **three Random Forest models** with
  `GridSearchCV` hyperparameter optimization: (1) seven clinical
  features, (2) metabolomic features, (3) clinical + metabolomic
  integration.
- Drafted a research proposal covering background, objectives,
  methodologies, and expected outcomes.

### Ivana Yang Lab — Professor, Biomedical Informatics

- Conducted an **exploratory software study** for the Schwartz | Yang
  Lab: processed EQTL and GWAS genomic data to model the two-level
  relationship SNPs → gene network → clinical phenotype.
- Cleaned and processed **trillions of data points** in Python + pandas
  using efficient data-structure and algorithmic patterns.
- Maintained reproducible analysis in Jupyter notebooks under Git
  version control.
